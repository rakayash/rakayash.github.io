KEY TECHNICAL PROJECTS

PhD research: Techno-economic feasibility for novel technology utilising Low-grade waste heat to hydrogen using reverse electrodialysis.

  • Estimated levelized cost of hydrogen for 1500 kg/day, identified economic barriers, potential opportunities for a novel waste to hydrogen process. Assessed environmental impacts of hydrogen production using Simapro and found membranes and spacers to be detrimental.
  • Developed a new method to measure permselectivity. Determined conductivity and permselectivity of 10 commercial ion-exchange membranes and estimated optimal values. Developed reverse electrodialysis cell for hydrogen production.

Master projects:

  • Thesis: 3D reconstruction of SOFC anode microstructure from FIB-SEM images. Quantified influence of thickness on triple-phase boundary (TPB) length and validated using electrochemical impedance and distributed relation times analysis.
  • Systems integration project: European Commission's FLEXI FUEL project. Designed control architecture and 3 tier safety aspects of a 1kW SOFC setup. Designed 1.2 kW Shell and Tube heat exchanger for exhaust gas cooling.
